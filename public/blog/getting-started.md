# Getting Started with Blood Draw Timer

Welcome to Blood Draw Timer! This guide will help you use our tool effectively during venipuncture procedures.

## Why Timing Matters

Proper tourniquet timing is crucial for:
- Patient comfort and safety
- Accurate laboratory results
- Preventing hemoconcentration
- Meeting quality standards

## Using the Tourniquet Timer

1. **Apply the tourniquet** to the patient's arm
2. **Click "Start Tourniquet"** immediately
3. **Watch the color indicator** as time progresses
4. **Release before 1 minute** for best results

## Understanding the Color Codes

- **Green (0-30 seconds)**: Safe zone, proceed normally
- **Yellow (30-45 seconds)**: Caution, be aware of time
- **Orange (45-60 seconds)**: Warning, prepare to release
- **Red (60+ seconds)**: Release immediately

## Collection Timer

Use the collection timer to:
- Track total procedure duration
- Document for quality assurance
- Monitor efficiency

## Best Practices

- Always verify patient identity before starting
- Select appropriate vein and equipment
- Release tourniquet as soon as blood flow is established
- Follow your facility's specific protocols

## Tips for Success

1. Position the timer where you can see it easily
2. Use on a tablet or phone at bedside
3. Reset between patients
4. Document times as needed for QA

Stay safe and accurate with every draw!
